97 emnlp-2010-Simple Type-Level Unsupervised POS Tagging


Source: pdf

Author: Yoong Keok Lee ; Aria Haghighi ; Regina Barzilay

Abstract: Part-of-speech (POS) tag distributions are known to exhibit sparsity a word is likely to take a single predominant tag in a corpus. Recent research has demonstrated that incorporating this sparsity constraint improves tagging accuracy. However, in existing systems, this expansion come with a steep increase in model complexity. This paper proposes a simple and effective tagging method that directly models tag sparsity and other distributional properties of valid POS tag assignments. In addition, this formulation results in a dramatic reduction in the number of model parameters thereby, enabling unusually rapid training. Our experiments consistently demonstrate that this model architecture yields substantial performance gains over more complex tagging â€” counterparts. On several languages, we report performance exceeding that of more complex state-of-the art systems.1
