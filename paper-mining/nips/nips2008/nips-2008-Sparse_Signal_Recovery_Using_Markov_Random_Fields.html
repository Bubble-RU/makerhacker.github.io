215 nips-2008-Sparse Signal Recovery Using Markov Random Fields


Source: pdf

Author: Volkan Cevher, Marco F. Duarte, Chinmay Hegde, Richard Baraniuk

Abstract: Compressive Sensing (CS) combines sampling and compression into a single subNyquist linear measurement process for sparse and compressible signals. In this paper, we extend the theory of CS to include signals that are concisely represented in terms of a graphical model. In particular, we use Markov Random Fields (MRFs) to represent sparse signals whose nonzero coefÔ¨Åcients are clustered. Our new model-based recovery algorithm, dubbed Lattice Matching Pursuit (LaMP), stably recovers MRF-modeled signals using many fewer measurements and computations than the current state-of-the-art algorithms.
