115 nips-2011-Hierarchical Topic Modeling for Analysis of Time-Evolving Personal Choices


Source: pdf

Author: Xianxing Zhang, Lawrence Carin, David B. Dunson

Abstract: The nested Chinese restaurant process is extended to design a nonparametric topic-model tree for representation of human choices. Each tree path corresponds to a type of person, and each node (topic) has a corresponding probability vector over items that may be selected. The observed data are assumed to have associated temporal covariates (corresponding to the time at which choices are made), and we wish to impose that with increasing time it is more probable that topics deeper in the tree are utilized.
